This website is an ADHD friendly version of the Yale School of Art website which is notoriously known for its over-complicated design which can be found on this link: https://www.art.yale.edu/.

The Yale School of Art is a graduate school that confers MFAs in Graphic Design, Painting/Printmaking, Photography, and Sculpture; and offers undergraduate-level art courses to Yale College students. Our website exists as an ongoing collaborative experiment in digital publishing and information sharing. On the official website the first block of text is presented on a neon yellow background and contains an additional button referred to as the 'editors details.' The text is all cramped into the left hand-side of the webpage which can be hard to navigate especially for an individual with ADHD. For the ADHD friendly version of this website, the text boxes have been separated, for an ADHD user it is best to only display the relevant chunks of text one at a time. The bright distracting yellow background has also been replaced by a muted green which relaxes the eyes and makes reading the text a more enjoyable process. The bionic reading method has also been implemented onto the text so that ADHD users can focus and read the text with greater ease.

It is important to balance texts with images. Images can be used to help gain a better understanding of the context behind the text.

There is no tool bar on the official website however, a note taking function as well as read aloud button would be very useful for such as website. It contains large paragraphs of information that could instead be read aloud to an ADHD user. Giving the user the opportunity to make notes will also ensure that they can retain important information.
