ADHD Friendly Website

Having a pastel blue, green or yellow background, with a minimalist layout is the best strategy to make a website accessible. Such a colour palette is easy on the eyes and helps users maintain their focus.

Attention deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) is among the most common neurobehavioral disorders presenting for treatment in children and adolescents. ADHD is often chronic with prominent symptoms and impairment spanning into adulthood. ADHD is often associated with co-occurring disorders including disruptive, mood, anxiety, and substance abuse. The diagnosis of ADHD is clinically established by review of symptoms and impairment.

Bionic reading is an effective method for reading and processing text. This method involves making the first few letters of a word bold, forcing your brain to fill in the rest of the word. Those with ADHD often find it hard to focus on text for prolonged periods of time however, this method could stimulate users.

Standard text should be in size 19, with a maximum of 70 characters per line. The line spacing should be 50% of the text size. The text colour should be a shade of dark grey and preferably in the font San Serif. It is essential that text is well spaced out as studies show that this helps users with ADHD sustain their attention. The colour dark grey is less straining on the eye and makes reading large amounts of text a more pleasurable experience.

It is important to balance texts with images. Images can be used to help gain a better understanding of the context behind the text.

The notes feature would allow users to write notes whilst browsing a website. This feature could be particularly useful for websites that contain a lot of important information that needs to be memorised. Studies have shown that people with ADHD tend to have issues with their working memory therefore, having a space where they can make notes is essential.

Studies have shown that those with ADHD are able to read more efficiently if some parts of a text is highlighted. An article published in the Journal of Abnormal Child Psychology suggested that those with ADHD were able to focus better on a text if it had an element of novelty to it. Hence, implementing a highlight feature would allow users to highlight appropriate areas of text that could then make the text easier to process.

This text-to-speech feature would allow ADHD users to listen to the text that they select. Large chunks of information tend to overwhelm those with ADHD thus, implementing this feature on a website would ensure that the user focuses on the text and enjoys the process.

Instead of having a settings menu that consists of an endless amount of features inside features, a better alternative would be to have the most essential gadgets for an ADHD user ready at their disposal, without having to go down the rabbit hole of trying to figure out the right settings and subsequently getting distracted. Most importantly these icons are intuitive and minimalistic, ensuring that an ADHD user can take advantage of the tools at hand.
